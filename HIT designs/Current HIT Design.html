INCIDENT OF HATE: For our purposes, we are defining an incident of hate as any bias, discriminatory or racist action(s) both verbal or physical taken against a person because of their perceived race. An incident of hate is definitely any hate crime but all incidents of hate are not necessarily hate crimes.

We use the federal definition of a hate crime. A hate crime includes crimes committed based on the victim’s perceived or actual race, color, religion, national origin, sexual orientation, gender, gender identity, or disability. A hate crime must involve a “crime” and it is often a violent crime, such as assault, murder, arson, vandalism, or threats to commit such crimes.
